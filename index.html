Insights

Key insights (observed patterns)

1) Usage intensity varies widely

The dataset shows a spread of low-to-high activity patterns, suggesting that “one-size-fits-all” engagement prompts may not land equally well across users.

2) Consistency matters more than peaks

Behaviour appears more sustainable when users show steady patterns rather than occasional spikes. This supports onboarding that reinforces small, repeatable habits.

3) Time-based rhythms can guide nudges

Day-to-day rhythms (e.g., weekday vs weekend differences) can inform when to deliver reminders, challenges or personalised summaries.

4) “At-risk” signals are measurable

Simple indicators like declining activity frequency or reduced logging consistency can be used as early-warning flags for retention interventions.

Note: Insights are descriptive and based on the available dataset; they are presented as hypotheses to validate with product instrumentation and controlled tests.

Action

Recommendations (proposed)

Segmented onboarding

Tailor onboarding prompts by usage intensity (light / moderate / heavy). Avoid overwhelming low-activity users.

Measure: onboarding completion, week-1 activation, WAU.

Habit loops

Reinforce consistent behaviours with streaks, light challenges and weekly summaries focused on progress, not perfection.

Measure: retention proxy, streak adoption, weekly summary opens.

Smart nudges

Use time-based rhythms to schedule reminders when users are most likely to engage (and suppress prompts when they are not).

Measure: nudge CTR, session starts, churn indicators.

Limitations

  • Public dataset: may not represent Bellabeat customers.
  • Descriptive analysis only: no causal inference.
  • No access to product event instrumentation, cohorts or paid acquisition context.

Next steps (iteration 2)

  • Define an event taxonomy (activation, habit loops, retention milestones).
  • Build cohort retention and funnel views (new vs returning, onboarding completion).
  • Design A/B tests for segmented onboarding and nudge timing.
